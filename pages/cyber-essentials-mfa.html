Cyber Essentials MFA: what to enable (and how to prove it)

In practice, MFA is one of the most common Cyber Essentials blockers for SMEs — not because teams disagree, but because MFA is enabled for some users and not enforced everywhere (especially for admin accounts).

mfa cyber essentials evidence

What you should protect with MFA

  • All admin accounts (non-negotiable)
  • Accounts with access to email, files, and business systems
  • Remote access and any management consoles

Common mistakes (that cause delays)

“Enabled” but not enforced

Some users are opted in, but there’s no policy forcing MFA across the tenant.

Admin accounts exempt

Legacy admin accounts or break-glass accounts are left outside the rules with no documented controls.

Multiple identity systems

M365 plus separate SaaS logins (accounting, CRM) without consistent MFA posture.

No evidence pack

MFA is configured, but nobody has screenshots/exports ready when the tender deadline hits.

Evidence to collect (minimum viable)

  • Screenshot(s) showing MFA enforcement policy
  • Screenshot(s) showing admin roles and MFA requirement
  • A short note: what exceptions exist (if any) and why

The goal is not perfect documentation — it’s fast, credible proof for your MSP/assessor.
