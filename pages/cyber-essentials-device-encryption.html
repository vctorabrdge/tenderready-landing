Device encryption for Cyber Essentials: what to do and what to screenshot

For UK SMEs, device encryption is usually easy to implement but surprisingly easy to fail on evidence. The fix: standardise the baseline and capture proof once.

bitlocker filevault evidence

What “good” looks like

  • All company laptops/desktops have disk encryption enabled (BitLocker/FileVault).
  • Recovery keys are stored securely (often in M365/Intune or an MSP system).
  • New devices are provisioned with encryption as standard (baseline).

Common pitfalls

Mixed fleet

Some machines are encrypted, others aren’t (especially older laptops).

No recovery key story

Encryption is on, but nobody can explain where recovery keys are held.

“One screenshot” fallacy

You have proof for one device, but can’t show it’s the standard.

Bring-your-own devices

BYOD and mobiles need a clear policy boundary (what’s allowed, what isn’t).

Minimum viable evidence pack

  • Screenshot: encryption enabled on a representative Windows device (BitLocker)
  • Screenshot: encryption enabled on a representative Mac device (FileVault)
  • Short note: how you ensure new devices follow the baseline
  • Short note: where recovery keys are stored (or MSP confirmation)
