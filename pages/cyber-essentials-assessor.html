Cyber Essentials assessor: what they do, what they need, and how to save time

This page targets cyber essentials assessor and is written for UK SMEs doing Cyber Essentials because a customer or tender requires it. The goal: reduce delays by making ownership and evidence obvious.

cyber essentials assessor SME evidence prompts

Quick answer

If you’re under a tender deadline, focus on two things: (1) the control is implemented consistently, and (2) you can produce a simple evidence pack quickly (screenshots/exports + a short note). That’s what prevents last-minute rework.

Last updated: 2026-02-20

What to do (SME practical steps)

  1. Identify ownership: who is responsible (in-house vs MSP)?
  2. Make it consistent: apply the control across devices/users, not just “most”.
  3. Capture evidence once: store 2–5 key screenshots/exports and a short note.
  4. Record exceptions: if something can’t comply yet, write the blocker + due date.

Evidence prompts (minimum viable)

Screenshot / export

  • One screenshot proving the setting/policy is enabled
  • One screenshot proving it’s enforced org-wide (where possible)
  • One export/list (users, admins, devices) if relevant

1-paragraph note

  • What the control is
  • Who owns it (person/MSP)
  • Where evidence lives
  • Known gaps + ETA

Common mistakes (and how to avoid them)

  • “Enabled” but not enforced: fix by using a single tenant-wide policy (e.g., MFA/CA) and documenting exceptions.
  • MSP owns it but you can’t prove it: fix by requesting a short MSP evidence pack early.
  • Evidence sprawl: fix by keeping an evidence index (file, date, control, note).
