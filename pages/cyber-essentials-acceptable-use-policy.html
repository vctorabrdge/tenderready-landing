Cyber Essentials acceptable use policy (AUP): the 1-page SME version

Cyber Essentials isn’t asking you to become a corporate security department. For SMEs, the best acceptable use policy is short, true, and easy to evidence.

acceptable use policy cyber essentials SME

What an AUP is for (in practice)

It sets clear boundaries for how staff use company accounts and devices. When an assessor/MSP asks “how do you control access and reduce common risks?”, a simple AUP helps you answer consistently.

Minimum viable AUP contents

  1. Accounts: no shared logins; each person uses their own account.
  2. MFA: required for company email and key systems.
  3. Passwords: use a password manager; don’t reuse passwords.
  4. Software: only install approved software; admin rights are controlled.
  5. Data: store company data in approved locations (M365/Google Drive), not personal accounts.
  6. Devices: screen lock on; report lost devices immediately.
  7. Phishing: don’t click suspicious links; report suspicious emails.

Keep it aligned with what you actually do today, then improve the process over time.

How to evidence your AUP

Evidence

  • A dated AUP doc (1 page)
  • Where it’s stored (shared drive/wiki)
  • How staff acknowledge it (email, onboarding checklist, HR tool)

Consistency

  • MFA enforcement screenshot
  • Password manager usage guidance
  • Admin rights policy and approval process
