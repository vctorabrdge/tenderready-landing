Cyber Essentials gap analysis (UK): what it is and how to do it fast

A gap analysis is the fastest way to avoid tender-deadline panic. It answers: what will stop us passing, who owns each fix, and what evidence do we need before we pay for assessment.

cyber essentials gap analysis blockers handoff pack

What a gap analysis produces

  • A list of controls with status (done / in progress / missing)
  • Named owners + due dates for each missing item
  • An evidence index (what you can show an assessor/MSP)
  • A short summary of blockers + ETA

When to do it

  • As soon as a tender/customer requires Cyber Essentials
  • Before you commit to paying for certification / CE+ testing
  • Before you book time with your MSP (so their time is high-leverage)

A practical 60–90 minute gap analysis workflow

  1. Collect facts: what devices you have, who is admin, what cloud you use (M365/Google), who manages the firewall (MSP vs in-house).
  2. Run the checklist across the five areas and mark each control as: Not started / In progress / Done / Not applicable.
  3. Assign fixes immediately (don’t let them become “someone needs to…”).
  4. Attach evidence as you go: screenshots, exports, a one-page policy note.
  5. Summarise blockers (the 2–5 items that actually stop pass) and set a realistic ETA for readiness.

If you’re construction/trades: treat this like a tender deliverable. Don’t aim for perfect documentation—aim for a clean handoff pack.

Common blockers we see in SMEs

MFA gaps

Admin accounts without enforced MFA, or mixed policies across M365 users.

Leavers process

Accounts still active, shared mailboxes, unclear ownership of access control.

Patching visibility

Updates may be “on”, but nobody can prove patch compliance quickly.

Firewall ownership

MSP manages it, but evidence isn’t packaged; you chase screenshots last minute.
